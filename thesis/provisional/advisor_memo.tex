\section*{6. MTU15 effect: critical/flat identification (ID15 and DA15)}

\subsection*{6.1 Specifications}

$u$ unit; $d$ date; $p$ period (hour pre-, quarter post-MTU15); $h$ clock-hour; $s$ IDA session; $T_r$ reform date. SEs clustered by date throughout; sample restricted to critical and flat hours.

\textbf{A. Per-curve} ($Y \in \{\sigma_p, N_{\text{eff}}\}$, in-band $|p_{\text{bid}}-\text{MCP}|\le 140$):
\[
Y_{u,d,p[,s]} = \alpha + \alpha_u + \beta\,\mathbf{1}\{d \ge T_r\} + \xi\,\mathbf{1}\{h \in \mathcal{C}\} + \theta\,\mathbf{1}\{d \ge T_r\}\cdot\mathbf{1}\{h \in \mathcal{C}\} + \varepsilon
\]

\textbf{B. Aggregate} ($P \in \{$OMIE clearing price, DA cleared MW$\}$):
\[
P_{d,p[,s]} = \delta_d + \xi\,\mathbf{1}\{h \in \mathcal{C}\} + \theta\,\mathbf{1}\{d \ge T_r\}\cdot\mathbf{1}\{h \in \mathcal{C}\} + \varepsilon \qquad (\text{date FE absorbs post})
\]

\textbf{C. Within-hour dispersion, post-only.} For $D \in \{D_{\text{price}}=\mathrm{sd}_q\bar p_q,\ D_{\text{qty}}=\mathrm{cv}_q m_q\}$ across the 4 within-hour quarters:
\[
D^{(\cdot,d,h)}_{\cdot} = \delta_d + \theta\,\mathbf{1}\{h \in \mathcal{C}\} + \varepsilon
\]

\paragraph{Reading (gaps for prose).}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{1pt}\setlength{\topsep}{2pt}
\item \textit{$\sigma_p$: positive significant DiD in both reforms for CCGT and Hydro --- MTU15 widens the in-band price ladder in critical hours. Per-tech heterogeneity: CCGT-led for ID15; Hydro-led for DA15 on $\sigma_p$, CCGT-led on $N_{\text{eff}}$.}
\item \textit{Prices: OPPOSITE signs across reforms. ID15 critical-hour IDA prices fall EUR\,$11/$MWh more than flat (granular intraday absorbs scarcity peaks); DA15 critical-hour DA prices rise EUR\,$27/$MWh more (granular day-ahead pricing extracts higher rents in scarce quarters).}
\item \textit{Quantity: DA15 critical-hour cleared MW falls $-2{,}115$ MW more than flat. Joint with the $+\text{EUR}\,27$ price effect $=$ textbook withholding signature.}
\item \textit{Spec C dispersion (post-only): every outcome is $3$--$7\times$ larger in critical than flat hours --- direct confirmation that MTU15 unlocks discrimination only where within-hour variance exists.}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{6.3 Robustness (one-line each)}

\textit{(All in \texttt{results/regressions/bid/mtu15\_critical\_flat/}.)} \textbf{(i) Date FE} (Spec B): DiD unchanged $\Rightarrow$ not driven by common date-level shocks. \textbf{(ii) Midday $\{11,\dots,14\}$ falsification} (Spec A pooled): $\sigma_p$ DiD $+1.10^{***}$ ID15 and $+2.26^{***}$ DA15 --- not zero but $\sim\!65\%$ of the critical magnitude; consistent with midday's intermediate within-hour variance, reads as a dose-response in variance rather than a placebo failure. \textbf{(iii) Per-CCGT-firm} (IB/GE/GN/HC): large heterogeneity (e.g.\ ID15 IB $\sigma_p$ $+6.46^{***}$, GN $N_{\text{eff}}$ $+1.84^{***}$); DA15 GN headline $\sigma_p$ $+35.0$ is a level-shift artefact (flat-hour mean collapsed $40.7\to 7.6$) and should be flagged, not read as within-hour MTU15. \textbf{To do:} extended pre windows; same-calendar-month; CCGT scarcity-tier split.
