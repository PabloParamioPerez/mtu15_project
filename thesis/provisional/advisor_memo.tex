\begin{abstract}
\noindent
The two MTU15 reforms turn within-hour bidding into a strategic margin, and the dispatchable fleet exploits it. We see this directly in the bid curves: in critical hours (morning ramp and evening peak) where within-hour residual demand actually varies, ladders widen substantially --- broadly under ID15 (CCGT, hydro, pump-storage) and more selectively under DA15 (hydro, CCGT). Wind, whose output spans all hours rather than concentrating in peaks, shows little critical-flat differential. The clearest quantity-side causal trace is on the day-ahead auction-cleared mix under DA15: CCGT cleared volume rises $\sim$59~GWh/day net of the 2024 same-calendar placebo (joint 95\% credible interval $[+43,+75]$) --- the size implied by finer granularity raising CCGT's chance of being marginal across the four quarters of a peak hour. Pump-storage independently corroborates the within-day arbitrage channel: under DA15 it shifts $+$237~MWh of day-ahead generation toward critical hours, concentrating its arbitrage exactly where granularity creates value. Clearing prices move in the directions the two-market bidding contrast would predict, but their placebo-net credible intervals bracket zero; we do not claim causal price magnitudes.
\end{abstract}

\section*{1. Setup: outcomes, windows, and seasonal control}

\begin{takeaway}
\emph{Takeaway.} We measure how MTU15 reshapes three families of outcomes: per-curve bid shape; aggregate clearing price and cleared energy; and post-only within-hour dispersion. Identification compares \emph{critical} clock-hours (those with the most within-hour load variation) to \emph{flat} clock-hours (overnight; the within-day control) across each reform's pre/post window. Outcomes whose pre/post comparison crosses different seasons --- chiefly DA15 prices and cleared quantities --- are additionally seasonally adjusted.
\end{takeaway}

\paragraph{Two reforms.} The Spanish wholesale electricity market shifted from a 60-minute to a 15-minute market time unit in two steps: \textbf{ID15} (2025-03-19, intraday auctions and continuous market) and \textbf{DA15} (2025-10-01, day-ahead). After each reform there are four periods per clock-hour; before, one. The \emph{operaci\'on reforzada} regulatory stance (REE's post-blackout reinforced operation, continuous from 2025-04-28) is a continuous confound that forces CCGT inclusion; tight pre/post windows around each reform date hold its level constant within each arm.

\paragraph{Variables.} Each (unit $u$, date $d$, period $p$) sell offer is a tranche ladder $\{(p_k, q_k)\}_{k=1}^{K_{u,d,p}}$ where $p_k$ is the bid price (EUR/MWh) and $q_k$ the quantity (MW per OMIE \emph{Potencia} field, \texttt{ficherosomie137.pdf} \S 5.1.4.2). Offers carry one of three structural types: \emph{simple} (a bare price--quantity ladder), \emph{minimum-income condition} (MIC), or \emph{block} (all-or-nothing across periods); CCGT is the outlier with the bulk of its offers in MIC or block form (\S 4, \S 5(d)). Let $\text{MCP}_{d,p}$ be the OMIE clearing price \emph{of the market in which the offer was submitted} (day-ahead offers, in the OMIE files cab/det; intraday-auction offers in session $s$, files icab/idet), and $h=140$ EUR/MWh the bandwidth. Define the in-band set, MW weights, and in-band weighted mean bid price:
\[
\mathcal{B}_{u,d,p} \;=\; \{k:\,|p_k-\text{MCP}_{d,p}|\le h\}, \qquad w_k \;=\; \frac{q_k}{\sum_{j\in\mathcal{B}_{u,d,p}} q_j}, \qquad \bar p_{u,d,p} \;=\; \sum_{k\in\mathcal{B}} w_k\,p_k.
\]

\emph{Per-curve} (in-band, MW-weighted; one value per (unit, date, period)):
\[
\sigma_{p,\,u,d,p} \;=\; \sqrt{\sum_{k\in\mathcal{B}_{u,d,p}} w_k\,(p_k-\bar p_{u,d,p})^2}, \qquad
N_{\text{eff},\,u,d,p} \;=\; \Big(\sum_{k\in\mathcal{B}_{u,d,p}} w_k^2\Big)^{-1}.
\]
$\sigma_p$ = price spread of the bid ladder within a single quarter; $N_{\text{eff}}$ = effective tranche count of that quarter (1 = a single block, large = many equal-sized tranches).

\emph{Aggregate}: $\text{MCP}_{d,p}$ is the OMIE clearing price (EUR/MWh); $Q_{d,p}=\sum_{u,k} q^{\text{cleared}}_{u,d,p,k}$ is the system DA cleared quantity per (date, period)\footnote{The OMIE assigned-power field is per-period instantaneous power (MW), invariant to period length, so one 15-min quarter and one 60-min hour at the same MW are directly comparable as rates. Aggregating MW across the four quarters of a clock-hour gives the stock of energy delivered in that clock-hour, $\text{MWh}_h = \sum_{q\in h}\text{MW}_q\,\Delta t_q$. Aggregate-quantity coefficients below are reported as MWh per clock-hour (numerically equal to the per-period mean MW because the integration is over 1 h, but the unit is energy, not power).}.

\emph{Within-hour dispersion} (post-only, used to calibrate the critical/flat partition in \S 2). The per-curve $\sigma_p$ and $N_{\text{eff}}$ describe the shape of a \emph{single} quarter's ladder. Here we instead summarize how price and shape vary \emph{across the four quarters} of a clock-hour --- a direct trace of within-hour discrimination, mechanically zero under MTU60. For each (unit, date, clock-hour $h$) cell with all four quarters present, let $q\in\{1,2,3,4\}$ index quarters, $\bar p_q=\sum_{k\in\mathcal{B}_q} w_k\,p_k$ the in-band MW-weighted mean bid price of quarter $q$, $\sigma_{p,q}$ and $N_{\text{eff},q}$ its per-curve metrics, $m_q=\sum_{k\in\mathcal{B}_q} q_k$ the in-band MW summed over the quarter, $\overline{m}=\tfrac{1}{4}\sum_q m_q$ the mean across the four quarters, and $\text{MCP}_q$ the quarter-$q$ clearing price:
\[
\begin{aligned}
D_{\text{price}} &= \mathrm{sd}_q(\bar p_q), &
D_{\text{qty}}   &= \mathrm{sd}_q(m_q)/\overline{m}, &
\text{SD}_{\text{price}} &= \mathrm{sd}_q(\text{MCP}_q), \\
D_\sigma &= \mathrm{sd}_q(\sigma_{p,q}), &
D_N      &= \mathrm{sd}_q(N_{\text{eff},q}).
\end{aligned}
\]
$D_{\text{price}}$, $D_{\text{qty}}$, $\text{SD}_{\text{price}}$ are across-quarter shifts in bid \textsc{level}, bid \textsc{volume}, and system clearing prices; $D_\sigma$, $D_N$ are across-quarter shifts in ladder \textsc{spread} and \textsc{tranche count}. All five are zero under the MTU60-equivalent strategy (identical bids in every quarter), so any positive value post-MTU15 is a direct trace of within-hour discrimination.

\paragraph{Seasonal control.} Bid-shape outcomes need none: $\sigma_p$ and $N_{\text{eff}}$ are MCP-centered and MW-weighted on the in-band ladder, so summer$\to$winter price-level seasonality does not transmit; the flat-hour control absorbs common-to-all-hours bid-shape seasonality and the placebo (\S 5(b)) checks for pre-existing trends in the critical$-$flat differential. For \emph{level outcomes} (prices, cleared energy), seasonality is controlled by the BSTS covariates (wind, solar, gas) plus a weekly seasonal cycle and the local-linear trend (\S 3.B), and residual cross-season trend is gauged by the 2024 same-calendar placebo. For the supporting pump-storage DiD (\S 4/5(c)) we use a same-calendar-month restriction (pre Oct--Dec 2024, post Oct--Dec 2025).

\section*{2. The critical/flat partition: where granular pricing has economic content}

\begin{takeaway}
\emph{Takeaway.} Within-hour discrimination has economic content only where within-hour residual demand actually varies. Critical hours (morning ramp + evening peak) are where MTU15 can bite; flat hours (overnight) are the within-day control. Post-MTU15 within-hour dispersion is much larger in critical than flat hours on every bid-level and bid-shape outcome we measure --- the partition correctly identifies where the reform lives.
\end{takeaway}

\paragraph{Intuition.} Granular pricing only has economic content where within-hour residual demand\footnote{Spanish-market convention calls this the \emph{hueco t\'ermico} (thermal gap) --- the load that thermal and other dispatchable generation must cover after subtracting wind and solar.} actually varies; if a clock-hour is internally flat, the four quarters look the same and there is nothing to discriminate on. We measure within-hour variation by $\sigma_{\text{within}}(h,d) = \mathrm{sd}_q[\text{load}-\text{wind}-\text{solar}]_{q,h,d}$ on 15-min residual-demand data\footnote{ENTSO-E Transparency Platform: A65 (actual total load) and the wind/solar component of A75 (actual generation per type, PSR codes B16, B18, B19); window 2023-01-01 to 2026-05-14.} and partition clock-hours by median $\sigma_{\text{within}}$:
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{1pt}
\item \textbf{Critical} $\mathcal{C}=\{5,\dots,8,\,16,\dots,22\}$: morning ramp + evening peak; $\sigma_{\text{within}}\in[522,1303]$ MW.
\item \textbf{Flat} $\mathcal{F}=\{1,2,3\}$: overnight; $\sigma_{\text{within}}\in[336,357]$ MW.
\item \textbf{Midday} $\{11,\dots,14\}$ (solar-marginal, $\sigma_{\text{within}}\in[468,502]$ MW): excluded from main specs; kept for falsification (\S 5(a)).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Formal calibration of the partition (post-only).} Let $D_{d,h}$ stand for any one of the five within-hour dispersion outcomes ($D_{\text{price}}$, $D_{\text{qty}}$, $\text{SD}_{\text{price}}$, $D_\sigma$, $D_N$) on date $d$, clock-hour $h$. Under MTU60 the within-hour quarter dimension does not exist, so $D_{d,h}$ is mechanically zero pre-reform; we use the post-MTU15 sample to \emph{describe} where within-hour dispersion lives across hour-classes. A date-FE regression is a convenient way to summarise the critical$-$flat differential:
\[
D_{d,h} \;=\; \delta_d \;+\; \theta\,\mathbf{1}\{h\in\mathcal{C}\} \;+\; \varepsilon,
\]
fit per outcome. $\theta$ is the post-MTU15 critical$-$flat dispersion gap. This is a descriptive contrast, not a causal estimate: operational ramp constraints in critical hours can mechanically generate within-hour SD, and the partition calibration cannot separate operational from strategic dispersion.

\section*{3. Identification: DiD for bid shape, BSTS for prices and quantities}

\begin{takeaway}
\emph{Takeaway.} Bid shape is identified by a critical/flat DiD: the (critical $-$ flat) shift across each cutover is the ATT under parallel trends. Clearing prices and per-tech cleared energy are identified by a Bayesian Structural Time Series counterfactual on a daily panel: conditional on the wind and solar renewable-supply covariates, the post-cutover series departs from the model's pre-trained extrapolation by the treatment effect; a 2024 same-calendar placebo gauges residual cross-season trend.
\end{takeaway}

$u$ unit; $d$ date; $p$ period; $h$ clock-hour; $s$ IDA session; $T_r$ reform date. Treatment status $D_{d,h} \equiv \mathbf{1}\{d\ge T_r\}\cdot\mathbf{1}\{h\in\mathcal{C}\}$; sample restricted to $h\in\mathcal{C}\cup\mathcal{F}$ for the DiD.

\textbf{A. Per-curve DiD for bid shape} ($Y\in\{\sigma_p, N_{\text{eff}}\}$, run per-tech):
\[
Y_{u,d,p[,s]} = \alpha_u + \beta\,\mathbf{1}\{d\ge T_r\} + \xi\,\mathbf{1}\{h\in\mathcal{C}\} + \theta\,D_{d,h} + \varepsilon.
\]
Under the parallel-trends assumption $E[Y(0)\mid \mathcal{C},\,d\ge T_r] - E[Y(0)\mid \mathcal{C},\,d<T_r] = E[Y(0)\mid \mathcal{F},\,d\ge T_r] - E[Y(0)\mid \mathcal{F},\,d<T_r]$, the DiD coefficient $\theta$ identifies the ATT on the critical-hour bid shape. Flat hours act as a low-exposure control (mechanically reachable post-cutover but with negligible within-hour residual demand variance, \S 2), giving the standard $2\times 2$ DiD without staggered-treatment negative weights (de Chaisemartin \& D'Haultf\oe uille, 2026, ch.~3). SEs are clustered by date.

\textbf{B. Bayesian Structural Time Series for prices and per-tech cleared energy.} Following Brodersen et al.\ (2015) as applied to the EPEX 15-min reform by M\"arkle-Hu\ss\ et al.\ (2017), the daily response $y_t$ (DA or IDA clearing price; per-tech daily cleared GWh) decomposes as
\[
y_t \;=\; \mu_t + \gamma_t + X_t'\beta + \varepsilon_t,
\]
where $\mu_t$ is a local linear trend with stochastic slope (default priors), $\gamma_t$ is a 7-period weekly seasonal cycle, $X_t = (\text{wind GWh}, \text{solar GWh}, \text{gas EUR})$ are exogenous renewable-supply and fuel-cost covariates, and $\beta$ is a spike-and-slab regression coefficient. M\"arkle-Hu\ss\ et al.\ used only wind and solar for EPEX; we add gas because Spain has meaningfully different gas exposure than Germany did in their window. Demand is excluded because it is itself partly an outcome and would induce simultaneity. Pre-cutover data train the model; the counterfactual at $t\ge T_r$ is $\hat y_t(0)\equiv E[y_t(0)\mid X_t,\,t< T_r]$, and the treatment effect at $t$ is $y_t - \hat y_t(0)$. The post-window average effect and its 95\% credible interval come directly from the Bayesian posterior.

Window choice follows the principle of holding the relevant regulatory regime constant across pre and post. For DA15 the maximum reforzada-constant pre-window is 2025-04-28 $\to$ 2025-09-30 (156 days, starting at reforzada onset). For ID15 the maximum regime-constant pre is 2024-06-14 $\to$ 2025-03-18 (278 days, post-IDA-reform 3-session architecture, includes the December 2024 ISP15 introduction whose effect on the daily-mean price is small and is absorbed by the regression coefficients on wind, solar and gas). M\"arkle-Hu\ss\ et al.'s shorter 80-day pre is reported as a robustness check (\S 5(c)); it gives larger magnitudes because the local-linear trend cannot extrapolate cross-season. Two placebos --- one on the same calendar 2024 for each reform --- falsify residual cross-season trend; placebo-net effects are the cleanest identification of the treatment.

The identifying restriction: conditional on $X_t$, the unobserved $y_t(0)$ for $t\ge T_r$ equals the pre-trained model's extrapolation. This rules out any unobserved level shift coincident with the cutover that is not predicted by $X_t$ or by the local-trend extrapolation, and the placebo gauges whether the placebo-window extrapolation is itself biased by seasonal trend.

\section*{4. Results: bid-shape widening, day-ahead CCGT auction-cleared scale-up, and directional price movements}

\noindent \textbf{Tech selection.} Spec A is run on the four technologies that bid into the in-band region with non-trivial frequency: CCGT (the focal scarcity-tier technology), hydro and hydro pump (cleanest from an omitted-variable-bias standpoint --- their marginal-cost stacks are insensitive to the gas--renewable mix), and wind (the dominant sell-side price-setter, included for consistency with Brown \& Reguant 2026). Solar PV is excluded because it bids effectively zero in-band scarcity-tier capacity; nuclear is excluded because its dispatch is predominantly bilateral.

\noindent \textbf{Per-tech caveats.} Wind within-hour $\sigma_p$ mixes sub-hourly forecast revisions with strategic ladder shaping, so any positive wind DiD reads as a price-taking baseline (see table notes). The offer-type composition concern (Figure 1, \S 5(d)) is CCGT-specific: hydro, hydro pump and wind are $\sim\!100\%$ simple offers in both arms, so their Spec A DiDs below are simple-only by construction.

\paragraph{What survives.} \textbf{(i) Bid-shape widening in critical hours} (Figure 1). The granularity effect is robust across both reforms. ID15 widens broadly (CCGT, hydro, and pump-storage all show positive $\sigma_p$ DiDs; cross-border control attenuates by only 8\%, \S 5(d)). DA15 is more selective: hydro $\sigma_p$ and CCGT $N_{\text{eff}}$ (broad across offer types) have clean placebos. Wind DiDs are small and read as a price-taking baseline.

\textbf{(ii) Day-ahead CCGT auction-cleared volume scales up under DA15.} Placebo-net CCGT cleared energy rises $+$59~GWh/day (joint 95\% credible interval $[+43,+75]$, propagating real and placebo posterior variance) --- the same channel as the bid-shape widening in (i): MTU15-DA lets CCGT discriminate finely within the hour, raising its expected acceptance probability across the four quarters of a peak hour. Hydro, solar and nuclear are essentially null once the 2024 same-calendar placebo is netted off (joint CIs bracket zero). Wind cleared also rises ($+$11~GWh/day net, joint CI $[-1,+23]$); the effect is borderline and wind shows little critical-flat differential in \S 2 (its output spans all hours rather than concentrating in peaks), so the granularity channel does not naturally explain it --- the $+$11 reads as residual cross-year dispatch variation the placebo does not absorb.

\textbf{(iii) Pump-storage within-day arbitrage.} Critical-clock-hour DA generation rises $+237$ MWh under same-calendar ($t=5.0$; baseline cross-season $+634$ is inflated by summer/winter demand-mix). Under same-calendar, generation scales up in both hour-classes but disproportionately in critical: flat $117\!\to\!259$ MWh, critical $645\!\to\!1018$ MWh; the pumping pattern stays concentrated in flat hours pre and post. The technology with the largest within-hour arbitrage value scaled up its critical-hour energy once it could bid quarter by quarter.

\textbf{(iv) Prices move directionally but joint credible intervals bracket zero.} Placebo-net point estimates are $-$20~EUR/MWh for IDA after ID15 (joint 95\% CI $[-50,+9]$) and $+$12~EUR/MWh for DA after DA15 (joint CI $[-15,+38]$). The signs track the two-market bidding contrast (\S 6): CCGT bids at marginal cost ($\sim$EUR 82--96/MWh) in IDA with $\sim$0\% withheld, so finer granularity lets cheaper marginal suppliers undercut in some within-hour quarters and drags the IDA average down; CCGT's two-tier scarcity-aware DA strategy means the auction-cleared mix shifts toward CCGT under DA15, raising the day-ahead level at the margin. The joint credible intervals (propagating the placebo's own posterior variance) bracket zero, however, so these price magnitudes do not stand as causal estimates --- the BSTS posterior for each side is individually significant, but the placebo-netted contrast is not.

\section*{5. Robustness: surviving findings hold; price effects do not}

\begin{takeaway}
\emph{Takeaway.} Pump-storage and bid-shape findings survive the standard threats; the BSTS DA15 CCGT cleared scale-up survives at joint 95\%; price effects do not.
\end{takeaway}

\paragraph{(a) Hour-class definition does not affect our results.} Three checks. \emph{Midday-vs-flat falsification}: $\sigma_p$ DiD is $\sim\!\!65\%$ of the critical-vs-flat magnitude --- dose-response in within-hour variance, not a placebo failure. \emph{Weekday-only}: at the baseline. \emph{Extended flat set} (add hour 0, then hour 23): pooled $\sigma_p$ survives every extension.

\paragraph{(b) ID15 results are window-invariant; DA15 CCGT $\sigma_p$ is the one window-sensitive finding.} \emph{Extended ID15 pre window} (back to 2024-06-14): $\sigma_p$ attenuates but the sign and significance survive. \emph{Pre-only placebo} (fake $T_r$ at the midpoint of each pre window): ID15 is clean, while the DA15 $\sigma_p$ placebo flags a pre-existing critical-flat trend --- so the DA15 bid-shape claim rests on hydro $\sigma_p$ and CCGT $N_{\text{eff}}$, where the placebo is clean.

\paragraph{(c) Seasonal control: BSTS placebo for level outcomes, same-calendar DiD for pump-storage; window-length sensitivity.} The BSTS placebo (\S 4 table) handles cross-season trend for prices and per-tech daily cleared energy: the placebo coefficient is the seasonal component and the placebo-net effect is the treatment. Pre-windows are chosen to hold the relevant regulatory regime constant (DA15: 156 d post-reforzada-onset; ID15: 278 d post-IDA-reform). M\"arkle-Hu\ss\ et al.'s shorter 80-day pre gives substantially larger raw magnitudes (e.g.\ ID15 IDA price $-$62 vs $-$38 long pre; DA15 nuclear $-$67 vs $-$18) but rests on a winter-only or summer-only pre, so the local-linear trend extrapolates a cross-season counterfactual blindly; we report the 80-day result as a window-length robustness check, the long pre as the headline. We also add gas as a covariate beyond the M\"arkle-Hu\ss\ wind+solar set because Spain has meaningfully different gas exposure than Germany. The pump-storage finding rests on a separate DiD with same-calendar restriction (pre $=$ Oct--Dec 2024, post $=$ Oct--Dec 2025), retaining $+237^{***}$ MWh ($t=5.0$) under same-calendar with weather controls, corroborated by a Fourier-SA cross-check ($+350$, $t=7.4$).

\paragraph{(d) None of the four candidate confound channels overturns the surviving findings.} \emph{Cross-border flows for ID15}: the same ID15 cutover moved pan-European IDA SIDC and XBID to 15 min, so this is a sibling treatment, not omitted-variable bias on $Y(0)$. The cross-border critical-flat DiD\footnote{ESIOS indicators 535 (FR imp), 536 (FR exp), 539 (PT imp), 540 (PT exp); net $=$ (FR\_imp $+$ PT\_imp) $-$ (FR\_exp $+$ PT\_exp).} is $+8\,965$ MWh ($t=+5.12$) for ID15 vs $+793$ ns for DA15; adding cross-border as a per-curve control attenuates ID15 $\sigma_p$ by only $8\%$ --- bid-granularity dominates. \emph{Reforzada} is absent from ID15 by construction and continuous over the DA15 cutover; its empirical signature is the same-cal DA15 CCGT cleared-energy decrease ($-1{,}363$ MWh), and the CCGT scarcity-tier split confirms it (withholding share $+3.7$pp$^{***}$, competing-tier $N_{\text{eff}}$ $+0.18^{***}$ --- reforzada parks capacity in the scarcity tier, MTU15 makes the competing-tier ladder more granular). \emph{CCGT offer-type composition} (\S 4, Figure 1) understates the pooled $\sigma_p$ DiD; simple-offer-only $+3.53^{***}$ is the cleaner estimate, $N_{\text{eff}}$ widens broadly. \emph{Per-CCGT-firm split}: the DA15 GN headline $+35.0$ is a flat-baseline collapse artefact (mean $40.7\!\to\!7.6$), not a within-hour MTU15 effect.

\section*{6. Two market-structure facts that frame the bidding evidence}

\paragraph{The two-tier CCGT bid curve and the day-ahead/intraday contrast.} Every Big-4 CCGT fleet runs a two-tier day-ahead offer: a low \emph{competing} tier near marginal cost ($\sim$\,EUR 100--110/MWh) and a high \emph{scarcity} tier above the $\sim$\,EUR 200 clearing-price ceiling --- shares above the ceiling: Naturgy 75\%, Endesa 22\%, Iberdrola 22\%, EDP-HC 19\%. Withholding is firm-wide, not a Naturgy story. The same fleets bid a single competitive tier at EUR 82--96/MWh with $\sim\!0\%$ withheld in the intraday auction, after Fase~I has cleared. A unit's marginal cost does not change between two auctions hours apart. The parked day-ahead capacity is recalled in Fase~I and paid pay-as-bid through a \emph{separate} restriction offer (procedure PO 3.2, BOE-A-2025-5342; settlement PO 14.4 ap.~20.1, BOE-A-2025-13076); the day-ahead two-tier curve is the geometry of the Fase~I-recall game, not cost-reflective bidding. MTU15-DA grants CCGT a within-hour degree of freedom over its competing tier. Through this lens, the $+$59~GWh/day CCGT auction-cleared scale-up in \S 4(ii) is the competing tier expanding into the four quarters of each peak hour.

\paragraph{Market concentration migrated from wholesale to ancillary services.} Wholesale-market HHI hovers around 1\,100 over 2016--2024 (below the moderate-concentration threshold of 1\,500) and falls mid-day when solar peaks (solar is less concentrated than the Big-3). Day-ahead restrictions upward HHI above 4\,000; deviations upward above 6\,000; tertiary upward around 5\,000. Median of 4 firms with accepted upward restrictions bids. As renewables eroded wholesale-market power, technical and locational requirements concentrated the ancillary markets that absorb their variability.
