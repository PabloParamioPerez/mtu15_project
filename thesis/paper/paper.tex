\subsection{Critical-vs-flat hours: empirical calibration}\label{sec:data:calibration}

Hours are classified by the \textbf{within-hour variance of residual demand},
\begin{equation}
   \sigma^2_{\text{within}}(h) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_d\!\left[\,\tfrac{1}{4}\sum_{q=1}^{4}\bigl(D_{h,q,d}-\bar{D}_{h,d}\bigr)^{2}\,\right],
   \qquad D_{h,q,d} = \text{demand}_{h,q,d} - \text{wind}_{h,q,d} - \text{solar}_{h,q,d}.
\end{equation}
\textbf{Critical hours}: high $\sigma^2_{\text{within}}$, 05:00--09:00 and 16:00--23:00 (eleven clock-hours). \textbf{Flat hours}: low $\sigma^2_{\text{within}}$, 01:00--04:00 (three clock-hours). Borderline hours are dropped. Full 24-hour ranking in Appendix~\ref{app:hour_metrics}.

OJO, IF WE INTRODUCE THE 11:00-15:00 PERIOD IN THE CONTROL, WE ARE MIXING THE OPERATIONAL AND STRATEGIC CHANNELS OF THE REFORM, BECAUSE THE OPERATIONAL EFFECT IS CONCENTRATED IN THOSE HOURS!!!

\subsection{Operational vs strategic use of MTU15 granularity}\label{sec:bid_evidence:operational_vs_strategic}

TWO CHANNELS BY WHICH A FIRM CAN ``USE'' WITHIN-HOUR GRANULARITY (SEE §\ref{sec:intro}):
\begin{itemize}
   \item OPERATIONAL: TRACK PHYSICAL VARIATION OF OWN PRODUCTION ACROSS QUARTERS (WIND / SOLAR FORECAST UPDATES, RAMPING). BID QUANTITY VARIES; BID PRICE STAYS AT MARGINAL COST.
   \item STRATEGIC: RE-PRICE ACROSS QUARTERS TO EXTRACT RENTS FROM WITHIN-HOUR DEMAND SWINGS. BID PRICE VARIES IN A DEMAND-CORRELATED WAY.
\end{itemize}

THE WITHIN-HOUR QUARTER-DISSIMILARITY TEST IN APPENDIX~\ref{app:quarter_dissim} (FRACTION OF (FIRM, UNIT, DATE, HOUR) CELLS WHERE THE FOUR 15-MIN QUARTERS' BIDS ARE NOT BIT-IDENTICAL) DOES NOT SEPARATE THESE TWO CHANNELS. THE CRITICAL--FLAT ASYMMETRY DOES: STRATEGIC RE-PRICING REQUIRES SYSTEMATIC WITHIN-HOUR DEMAND VARIATION (CRITICAL HOURS BY OUR DEFINITION) AND SHOULD BE ABSENT WHEN SUCH VARIATION IS LOW (FLAT HOURS). OPERATIONAL REFINEMENT IS DRIVEN BY OWN-PRODUCTION VARIABILITY, WHICH IS NOT TIED TO HOUR-CLASS.

\subsection{Strategic withholding and intraday upward repositioning}\label{sec:results:q2}

\subsection{Within-hour quarter dissimilarity test}\label{app:quarter_dissim}

Falsification test for the aggregated Q1--Q4 plots: a perfect overlap in the cross-cell average could mask cell-level variation if its direction is idiosyncratic. The diagnostic operates at the (firm, unit, date, hour) cell level, before any aggregation.

\paragraph{Construction.} Each quarter's bid in a cell is a discrete measure on the price axis: each tranche $(p_k, q_k)$ contributes a point mass $q_k$ MWh at price $p_k$ EUR/MWh, after integer-rounding the price. Let $\mu_i$ denote the bid measure for quarter $i \in \{1,2,3,4\}$, with cumulative distribution
\[
   F_i(p) \;=\; \sum_{k:\,p_k \leq p} q_k,
\]
which is exactly the bid-curve step function plotted in §\ref{sec:bid_evidence:perfirm}: at price $p$ on the y-axis, $F_i(p)$ is the cumulative MWh offered up to that price.

\paragraph{Dissimilarity between two quarters.} For an ordered pair $(i, j)$, we compute the L1 area between the cumulative bid curves over the union of all bid prices present in either quarter:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wasserstein}
   W_1(\mu_i,\mu_j) \;=\; \int_{p_{\min}}^{p_{\max}} \bigl| F_i(p) - F_j(p) \bigr|\, dp \;=\; \sum_{k=1}^{K-1} \bigl| F_i(p_{(k)}) - F_j(p_{(k)}) \bigr|\cdot \bigl( p_{(k+1)} - p_{(k)} \bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\{p_{(1)} < p_{(2)} < \dots < p_{(K)}\}$ is the sorted union of distinct bid prices from both quarters and $[p_{\min}, p_{\max}] = [p_{(1)}, p_{(K)}]$ is the resulting integration domain. Computation handles the union grid in $O(K \log K)$ via a sort/merge of the two ordered price lists; see \texttt{scripts/analysis/bid/quarter\_dissimilarity\_ot.py}.

\paragraph{Wasserstein interpretation.} Equation~\ref{eq:wasserstein} is the closed-form expression of the 1-D Wasserstein-1 distance (Earth Mover's Distance) for probability measures on $\mathbb{R}$: $W_1(\mu, \nu) = \int |F_\mu - F_\nu|$ \citep{vallender1973}. Strictly, the equality requires the two measures to share total mass. In our setting, two quarters may submit slightly different total volumes ($\sum_k q_{i,k} \neq \sum_k q_{j,k}$), in which case Equation~\ref{eq:wasserstein} is the natural \emph{unbalanced} extension --- the L1 area between two non-decreasing step functions over the observed price range. This still captures (i) horizontal shifts of the curve (price re-pricing), (ii) vertical shifts (quantity re-allocation), and (iii) total-volume changes, in a single non-negative number. Magnitudes (in MWh$\cdot$EUR/MWh) are not directly interpretable, but the metric serves the role we need: \textbf{$W_1 = 0$ iff the two bid multisets are identical}, and $W_1 > 0$ iff there is any difference.

\paragraph{Per-cell statistic and flag.} For each cell with all four quarters present we compute the maximum pairwise dissimilarity:
\[
   D \;=\; \max_{1 \leq i < j \leq 4} W_1(\mu_i, \mu_j).
\]

\subsection{Triple-difference: pivotality as moderator}\label{app:firm_ddd}

% TODO prose. Rationale: in the within-day DiD (Eq. 1) hours are the treatment
% partition (critical vs flat) and pivotal firms enter only as the sample
% restriction. Here we pool both pivotal and non-pivotal firms and run a
% triple-difference: the headline coefficient is the (critical × post × pivotal)
% interaction, which identifies the moderating effect of market power on the
% reform's response in critical hours. b123 = b3(pivotal) - b3(non-pivotal)
% by construction.
%
% Identifying assumption: unconditional parallel trends on the second-level
% DiD -- in the absence of the reform, the within-day critical-flat
% differential would have evolved in parallel between pivotal and non-pivotal
% firms. We do NOT invoke conditional parallel trends in the Sant'Anna sense:
% CPT is an assumption about counterfactual evolution given X, which justifies
% the OR / IPW / doubly-robust estimators of Sant'Anna and Zhao (2020), not
% OLS with X on the right-hand side. The X variables in specs (2)-(4) below
% are standard regression-style controls that absorb day-level shocks
% (renewables and demand), not a CPT defense. Their role is to verify
% robustness: the headline beta_123 is essentially invariant to whether they
% are included or excluded, which says the unconditional PTA passes the
% no-correlated-confounders sniff test for these specific observables.

\begin{multline}\label{eq:ddd}
   q_{2,fudh} = \alpha + \beta_1\,\mathrm{crit}_h + \beta_2\,\mathrm{post}_d + \beta_3\,\mathrm{piv}_f \\
   {}+ \beta_{12}\,\mathrm{crit}_h\,\mathrm{post}_d + \beta_{13}\,\mathrm{crit}_h\,\mathrm{piv}_f + \beta_{23}\,\mathrm{post}_d\,\mathrm{piv}_f \\
   {}+ \beta_{123}\,\mathrm{crit}_h\,\mathrm{post}_d\,\mathrm{piv}_f + \gamma_f + \delta_{\mathrm{DOW}(d)} + \boldsymbol{\theta}'\,X_{dh} + \varepsilon_{fudh},
\end{multline}
where $X_{dh} = (\text{wind}_{dh},\,\text{solar}_{dh},\,\text{demand}_{dh})$ in GW.
