\section{IDA pre/post-blackout near-clearing dissimilarity}\label{sec:prov:ida_pre_post}
% =====================================================================

New empirical test (2026-05-16). For each \texttt{(firm, unit, date, hour)} cell of CCGT IDA bids, compute the kernel-weighted within-hour quarter dissimilarity $D_w$ (Epanechnikov kernel, $h = 20$~EUR/MWh, centred on the hour-mean IDA clearing price). Compare two regimes that hold the MTU15-IDA clock constant:

\begin{itemize}
   \item \textbf{Pre-blackout}: 2025-03-19 $\to$ 2025-04-27 (40 days; post-MTU15-IDA, reforzada-free).
   \item \textbf{Post-blackout}: 2025-04-28 $\to$ 2025-09-30 (155 days; reforzada active throughout).
\end{itemize}

The hypothesis being tested: under reforzada, REE forces CCGT dispatch via post-clearing intervention, so the marginal-bid leverage on the clearing price is reduced and within-hour granularity exploitation should weaken (pre $>$ post). The data reject this prediction.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{\textbf{IDA quarter-dissimilarity, pre vs post-blackout, CCGT.} Percentage of (firm, unit, date, hour) cells with $D_w > 0$ (i.e., quarters differ within the $\pm 20$~EUR/MWh band around hour-mean IDA clearing price). \emph{Pre-blackout}: 2025-03-19 $\to$ 2025-04-27 (40 days, MTU15-IDA active, reforzada-free). \emph{Post-blackout}: 2025-04-28 $\to$ 2025-09-30 (155 days, reforzada active).}
\label{tab:ida_pre_post}

\cmidrule(lr){2-4}\cmidrule(lr){5-7}

\midrule
IB &  9.4 &  7.0 & $+2.4$        & 18.6 &  6.2 & $+12.4$ \\
GE & 12.4 &  8.1 & $+4.3$        & 36.1 & 21.4 & $+14.7$ \\
GN & 29.6 &  9.2 & $\mathbf{+20.4}$ & 50.3 & 36.2 & $+14.1$ \\
HC & 27.9 & 20.0 & $+7.9$        & 32.3 & 20.3 & $+12.0$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\textit{Reading.} (i) Pre-blackout, only Naturgy shows a clean critical-vs-flat strategic gap; the other three are noisy. (ii) Post-blackout, all four firms show a $+12$ to $+15$ percentage-point gap — granularity exploitation broadens. (iii) The directional prediction (pre $>$ post) is rejected for every firm. (iv) Flat-hour rates post-blackout are non-trivial (6--36\%), which the kernel-weighted falsification ought to keep at zero if all variation were strategic; the elevated flat-hour baseline is itself a reforzada signature (overnight CCGT commitments creating quarter-level operational adjustments).

A coherent alternative narrative: reforzada \emph{amplifies} the inframarginal volume on a forced-up CCGT (because REE pays Pmin out of merit at the unit's own price-of-restrictions offer), so a higher DA / IDA clearing price is worth proportionally more on the forced block. The strategic incentive to push the clearing price via within-hour bid shaping rises, not falls.

\paragraph{Source data.} \texttt{scripts/analysis/bid/quarter\_dissimilarity\_ida.py}; outputs at \texttt{results/regressions/bid/quarter\_dissimilarity\_ida/}.
