\section{1. Reminder}
% ============================================================

\begin{frame}{Objective and concrete research questions}
  \begin{block}{Aim}
    Empirically assess the impact of Spain's 2024--2025 \textbf{MTU15 reform sequence} on market outcomes and firm strategic behaviour, and extend the theoretical framework to capture the mechanism.
  \end{block}
  \footnotesize
  Five concrete sub-questions:
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item Does the \textbf{asymmetric-clock window} (DA60 + ID15 + ISP15) generate a measurable dual-pricing redistribution from BRPs to the TSO? Does it close at MTU15-DA?
    \item Does finer market-time-unit granularity \textbf{reduce strategic forward-quantity withholding} (Allaz--Vila / Ito--Reguant prediction)?
    \item Does the reform create \textbf{heterogeneous winners and losers} across firms and technologies?
    \item Does the per-segment incidence of imbalance settlement match a \textbf{Pigouvian benchmark}?
    \item Has \emph{operación reforzada} (post-blackout regime) broken the separability of the reform sequence?
  \end{enumerate}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{The 2024--2025 reform sequence}
  \begin{columns}[T,onlytextwidth]
    \column{0.50\textwidth}
    \footnotesize
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Traditionally MTU was \textbf{60 min} everywhere.
      \item Three stages:
        \begin{itemize}
          \item \textbf{Dec 2024 --- ISP15}: imbalance settlement $\to$ 15-min.
          \item \textbf{Mar 2025 --- ID15}: intraday $\to$ 15-min.
          \item \textbf{Oct 2025 --- DA15}: day-ahead $\to$ 15-min.
        \end{itemize}
      \item Two transitional regimes where \emph{settlement} and \emph{trading} clocks differ.
      \item Plus: post-2025-04-28 blackout $\Rightarrow$ \emph{operación reforzada} (REE's reinforced-operation regime), continuous since.

\section{2. What I have done}
% ============================================================

\begin{frame}{Data pipeline (1/2) --- what each layer lets us see}
  \footnotesize
  Answering the five research questions requires reconstructing the \emph{entire trace} of an electricity transaction --- from a firm's first bid in the day-ahead auction to the final cash flow at imbalance settlement. So I built the data infrastructure end-to-end.
  \vspace{4pt}
  \begin{block}{To see prices and the reform itself --- OMIE (Iberian market operator)}
    Day-ahead clearing prices, intraday auction prices (per session), and the entire continuous-market price record at native granularity (MTU60 pre-2025-03-19, MTU15 after). This is what tells me \emph{whether} the reform moved equilibrium prices, when, and by how much.
  \end{block}
  \begin{block}{To see strategic conduct --- OMIE bid-level data}
    Every tranche of every offer ever submitted to the day-ahead and IDA auctions, plus the full XBID order-book and matched-trade record. This is what lets me test Allaz--Vila / Ito--Reguant / Hortaçsu--Puller predictions at the level the theory speaks --- the individual bid function.
  \end{block}
  \begin{block}{To see imbalance and the system response --- ENTSO-E + REE/ESIOS}
    \emph{ENTSO-E TP}: imbalance volumes and prices, FRR settlement (A87), generation by type and unit, load and renewable forecasts. All fundamentals controls and forecast errors come from here. \\
    \emph{REE/ESIOS}: per-firm and per-segment settlement, real-time RT prices, balancing offers. \\
    \textbf{Caveat:} ESIOS \emph{API} access still pending --- 3-week wait. Public archive endpoints work; per-BRP feeds blocked until the token clears.
  \end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Data pipeline (2/2) --- the dimension of what we have}
  \footnotesize
  Putting it together, the panel is unusually rich for a Master's thesis. The whole point is that strategic mechanisms operate at sub-hourly granularity --- aggregating ex ante would erase exactly what we are trying to identify.
  \begin{block}{Scale}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{$\sim$8 years} of coverage (2018-01 $\to$ 2026-02), at native MTU60/MTU15 granularity throughout.
      \item \textbf{$\sim$413 GB raw} + \textbf{$\sim$198 GB processed} on dedicated external SSD; 25+ data families with their own parsers.
      \item \textbf{$\sim$5.7 billion rows} on the OMIE side alone, headlined by:
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Continuous-market final hourly programs (PHFC): \textbf{3.3 billion rows}.
        \item IDA bid tranches (IDET): 314M rows. DA bid tranches (DET): 172M rows.
        \item Day-ahead bilateral programs (PDBF): 166M rows --- recently added; opens the bilateral-channel mechanism.
        \item Aggregate DA supply--demand curves: 341M rows.
        \item XBID order book: 163M orders, 32M matched trades.
      \end{itemize}
    \end{itemize}
  \end{block}
  \begin{block}{Why it matters for identification}
    Most papers that study Spanish wholesale markets rely on \emph{published} hourly price series ($\sim$70k rows). Working at the bid-tranche / firm-ISP level lets me cluster at the right grain (week-of-sample, day, or unit), control for forecast errors at native frequency (Fabra--Imelda style), and run robustness checks on samples in the millions of observations rather than hundreds. This is where the project's empirical credibility lives.
  \end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Empirical strategy}
  \footnotesize
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{No clean external counterfactual} --- most comparable EU markets adopted MTU15 already and differ structurally.
    \item Reduced-form before/after with rich controls:
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Fundamentals: load, wind + solar (actual + forecast errors), fuel/CO$_2$.
        \item Calendar: cal-month FE, day-of-week, hour-of-day; year FE where appropriate.
        \item Reform-date and \emph{reforzada} indicators.
      \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Same-calendar-month robustness} mandatory for any cross-regime claim (Spain is highly seasonal).
    \item \textbf{Cluster-robust SE} by date or week-of-sample (Cameron--Miller G $\geq 30$ rule).
    \item \textbf{Cross-country placebo}: France DA prices flat across Spanish reform dates.
    \item \textbf{Structural-time-series counterfactual} (Brodersen et al.~2015): ongoing.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Headline findings}
  \footnotesize
  \begin{block}{1. System-layer settlement transfer}
    During the asymmetric-granularity window (Dec~2024--Sep~2025) the BRP$\to$TSO net transfer was \textbf{+€1{,}094.9M cumulative} vs same-calendar pre-reform baseline (bootstrap CI $[-90,+73]$M $\Rightarrow \approx 15\times$ upper bound). Collapses to \textbf{€7.4M/mo at MTU15-DA} ($12\times$ drop). Regulatory \emph{redistribution}, not deadweight.
  \end{block}
  \begin{block}{2. Mechanism --- forecast-error pass-through to imbalance}
    Pooled regression, daily grain: net per-regime slope $\beta$ is $\sim 0$ pre-reform, $\sim 0$ at 3-session IDA, $-0.03$ at ISP15-win, \textbf{$+0.09$ at DA60/ID15} (uniquely elevated), $\sim 0$ at DA15/ID15. Robust to year FE.
  \end{block}
  \begin{block}{3. Firm-level market power (Iberdrola)}
    Synthetic-firm replication \emph{à la} Ciarreta--Espinosa: \textbf{Iberdrola alone $\sim$€820M} day-ahead cleared-price-difference rent post-MTU15-IDA (98\% of joint Big-4); hydro 64\% / CCGT 36\%. \textbf{Regime-invariant.} Behavioural complement: Big-4 voluntary IDA repositioning ($q_2$, à la Ito--Reguant) collapses progressively, recovers at DA15/ID15; robust under canonical Fabra--Imelda controls.
  \end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Tests of theoretical predictions --- with the literature}
  \footnotesize
  \textbf{What worked:}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Ito \& Reguant (2016, AER)} sequential markets, market power, arbitrage: predicted $q_2$ collapse if firms react to a finer spot grid \,$\to$\, \emph{confirmed} (regime coefficients vary $<0.1\%$ across spec battery).
    \item \textbf{Ciarreta \& Espinosa (2010, J Regul Econ)} synthetic-firm method \,$\to$\, \emph{replicated and extended} (€820M IB rent; survives sensitivity tests).
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{2pt}
  \textbf{What did not work:}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Allaz \& Vila (1993, JET)} forward markets dispel rents: my test was the slope $\partial \Delta Q_{IDA}/\partial q_{DA}$ \,$\to$\, \emph{rejected as a test} ($Q_{actual} \approx q_{DA} + \Delta Q_{IDA}$ is a mechanical accounting identity, not a structural slope). The surviving Allaz--Vila evidence comes from the Ito--Reguant $q_2$ trajectory above.
    \item \textbf{Hortaçsu \& Puller (2008, RAND)} sophistication: implied Cournot--FOC Lerner vs realised marginal-bid markup \,$\to$\, \emph{strategic-conduct interpretation rejected} (implied $\approx 0.93$ vs realised $\approx 0.34$ for the leading firm; implied is formula-mechanical).
    \item \textbf{Bushnell (2003, JIE)} hydro market power via reservoir shadow-price \,$\to$\, \emph{mechanism rejected} (no reservoir gradient; placebo stronger than treatment). The IB hydro Q4 concentration is real but not classical Bushnell.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\section{4. Difficulties}
% ============================================================

\begin{frame}{Difficulty 1 --- identification under multiple confounds}
  \footnotesize
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Each reform date has at least one competing event:
      \begin{itemize}
        \item \emph{Dec 2024}: European gas-price normalisation.
        \item \emph{Mar 2025}: CNMC eliminates the bilateral-contract opportunity-cost rule simultaneously.
        \item \emph{Apr 28 2025} blackout $\to$ \emph{operación reforzada} (regime overlay continuous since).
        \item \emph{Oct 2025}: partial overlap with reforzada.
      \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Strategy adopted:}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item \emph{Market-chain placement}: reforzada acts \emph{after} the day-ahead clearing $\Rightarrow$ DA-side outcomes face it only via expectations.
        \item \emph{Reforzada-held-constant pairings}: ISP15-win $\to$ DA60/ID15-PRE-blackout (intraday reform); DA60/ID15-POST $\to$ DA15/ID15 (DA reform).
        \item Same-cal-month + cross-country placebo + bootstrap CIs.
      \end{itemize}
    \item Honest record of \textbf{14 dead causal-identification attempts} (DiD, wind-IV, profile-matching) --- rejected after placebo tests.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Difficulty 2 --- a structural break in Q1 2024 we cannot yet explain}
  \begin{columns}[T,onlytextwidth]
    \column{0.5\textwidth}
    \footnotesize
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Big-4 \textbf{nuclear bilateral-contract share} stable at 80--95\% across 2018--2023.
      \item Drops sharply in \textbf{Feb--Mar 2024}, three months \emph{before} any MTU15 reform.
      \item Hydro shows the same Q1-2024 break (auction-side growth); CCGT unaffected.
      \item Concurrent with the \textbf{end of the Iberian gas-cap (Dec 2023)} and the first major renewable-saturation event in Spain (negative DA prices appear Apr 2024).
      \item Regression on negative-price-share (exogenous proxy, with unit/cal-month FE): nuclear $\beta=-3.05$ (R$^2=0.13$); the implied magnitude matches the observed drop. Hydro $\beta\approx 0$.
    \end{itemize}
    \footnotesize \textbf{Mechanism unidentified}; reframes how I read 2024 pre-reform data.

\end{frame}
