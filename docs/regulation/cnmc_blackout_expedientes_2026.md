# CNMC sanctioning expedientes opened in the framework of the 2025-04-28 blackout investigation

**Source:** [CNMC — Expedientes de energía sancionadores en tramitación](https://www.cnmc.es/listado/expedientes_energia_sancionadoras_en_tramitacion/block/0). Press release 2026-04-17 (PDF saved at `cnmc_NP_incoaciones_28A_20260417.pdf` in this directory).

**Captured:** 2026-04-27.

## Context (from the press release)

> *"Tras el incidente eléctrico del 28 de abril de 2025, la Comisión Nacional de los Mercados y la Competencia (CNMC) inició varios expedientes informativos con el fin de analizar las circunstancias del cero eléctrico y determinar los hechos relevantes bajo la perspectiva de la normativa sectorial eléctrica."*
>
> *"Como resultado de las investigaciones, la CNMC ha detectado **diversos indicios de incumplimiento, algunos mantenidos durante periodos prolongados de tiempo**, que habrían afectado al funcionamiento del sistema eléctrico y podrían ser constitutivos de infracciones administrativas."*
>
> *"Estos expedientes sancionadores se incoan en el marco de las investigaciones del incidente del 28 de abril de 2025. **No obstante, los hechos objeto de estos procedimientos no implican, por sí mismos, la atribución del origen o causa del apagón a las empresas afectadas, dado que el incidente respondió a un origen multifactorial.**"*

Key claim: violations are "**maintained over prolonged periods of time**" — i.e., not just on April 28, but **chronic** non-compliance over preceding years (2023+ per news reporting). Procedure duration: 9–18 months depending on severity.

## Why this matters for the thesis

CNMC alleges, against IB and other firms:
1. **Article 64.37**: chronic unauthorized capacity reduction (very serious — only 3 firms targeted: IB Cofrentes, Almaraz AIE, REE).
2. **Article 65.8**: chronic voltage-control / reactive-power non-compliance (~40 expedientes across IB, Endesa, Naturgy, Repsol, etc., over "prolonged periods" = 2023+).
3. ENTSO-E identified **ineffective voltage control** as the proximate blackout mechanism.

This regulatory framing supports a thesis-grade economic mechanism:

> **Tension between economic optimization and system-stability provision under vertical-integration market power.** Reactive power and voltage control are public-good ancillary services with weak revealed enforcement. Under economic pressure (post-coal-phase-out, solar mid-day saturation, low wholesale prices), dispatchable firms — especially nuclear, which is inframarginal — face a quiet incentive to under-provide ancillary services to economize on opportunity costs. When the system stability degrades, REE compensates via "operación reforzada" (forced commitment of CCGT + dispatchable hydro), and the SAME vertically-integrated firm (Iberdrola) captures the resulting windfall on its CCGT + hydro fleet. **Same firm potentially profits from both sides of its alleged misconduct** — moral hazard under vertical integration with cross-subsidy.

## Empirical tests this enables

1. **Nuclear availability factor 2018-2025**: compare cleared MW vs nameplate by firm × month/year. Article 64.37 evidence requires showing chronic shortfall.
2. **IB nuclear schedule vs IB CCGT/hydro dispatch correlation**: if IB nuclear was at low output AND IB CCGT/hydro at high output simultaneously (especially during operación reforzada), the cross-subsidy story has empirical support.
3. **Mid-day economic pressure**: did nuclear reduce output during hours when wholesale price < (proxy) marginal cost (S9 evidence)?
4. **Operación reforzada beneficiary attribution**: who received the forced-commitment revenue? IB likely captured most.
5. **Per-firm per-period reactive-power dispatch (if data available)**: direct test of Article 65.8 allegation.
